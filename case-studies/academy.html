Training gap

The training gap

The opportunity was not to replace Academy's instructional design work, but to make product knowledge easier for Academy, Support, and customer education teams to translate into accurate, learner-ready training material.

Complex workflows were hard to translate into training

Product areas required users to understand permission differences, setup steps, mobile behavior, workflow consequences, and exceptions — context that source documentation didn't always spell out clearly for a training audience.

Role

My role

My role was technical writing and Academy support. I supported Academy work by creating refined source content, providing SME context, persona framing, and weighing which product details were essential, so it was easier to translate into learning material because the underlying documentation was already structured and accurate.

Principle

Documentation helps users complete tasks. When my source documentation is structured around real personas and real decisions, it becomes training-ready, not just reference-ready.

Approach

How I supported the training rollout

Start with user roles

Clarify who needed the training: new customers, support agents, Academy partners, or internal teams, and write source content with that persona in mind from the start.

Support accuracy through SME review

Use product context, technical review, and documentation standards to keep training aligned with the real product before it reached learners.

Reflection

What this taught me

Training and documentation aren't separate disciplines — they're two audiences reading the same underlying knowledge. When I write source documentation with a learner's questions in mind, everyone downstream moves faster.
