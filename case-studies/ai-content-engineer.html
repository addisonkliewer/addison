Two AI projects, one content systems story

I worked on two related AI initiatives, but each one addressed a different problem. The first focused on whether AI could find the right answer. The second focused on whether it could responsibly create a useful first draft.

My core story

I helped make organizational knowledge more accurate, searchable, reusable, and safe to scale across human and AI workflows.

Project 1

AI Search Optimization

My teammate and I analyzed real Doc360 search behavior to improve how AI surfaced documentation answers for common product questions.

Problem

Users were asking specific product questions, but our AI search responses were not always successful in answering their questions. We found weak points appeared across missing content, inconsistent terminology, metadata, taxonomy, and article structure.

What we found

We found that AI search was only as useful as the documentation behind it. By improving content structure, terminology, and metadata, we helped AI return more useful answers for both internal teams and customers.

Responsible AI position

AI usage should accelerate trusted knowledge, not add or invent new unverified product knowledge.

Responsible AI documentation principles

AI accelerates expertise

AI can help writers move faster, but it does not replace product judgment or editorial review, or continuously improve by itself.

Good AI starts with good documentation

Structured, consistent, well tagged documentation improves both search and generation quality.

AI should retrieve trusted knowledge

AI is strongest when grounded in verified product information, not when asked to invent undocumented or unverified behavior.

Human review and verification protects user trust

Writers remain responsible for accuracy, clarity, product intent, and the final published experience.

Reflection

What this taught me

The biggest lesson for me was that AI does not magically clean up content debt. It exposes it. When documentation is inconsistent, poorly organized, or missing context, those same problems show up in the AI's output.

I also saw the opposite. When my content is clearly structured, consistently named, properly tagged, and grounded in verified product knowledge, my work becomes useful far beyond the help center. It can support search, drafting, customer support, release communication, and product education.

"For me, the real focus was not simply using AI. It was building content systems that kept product knowledge accurate, reusable, easy to find, and safe to scale."
