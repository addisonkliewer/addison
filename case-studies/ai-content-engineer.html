Project 1

AI search optimization

As the documentation library scaled past 800 articles, findability became the bottleneck. I applied information architecture and tagging principles to make content easier for both users and AI retrieval systems to surface accurately, improving self-serve search success.

Project 2

AI-assisted documentation workflow

I piloted AI tools to accelerate first drafts and tagging suggestions, always with human-in-the-loop review. The goal was speed without sacrificing accuracy, clarity, or brand voice — AI as a drafting partner, not a replacement for editorial judgment.

Responsible AI documentation principles

Human review is non-negotiable

Every AI-assisted draft goes through the same SME and editorial review as any other article — no exceptions.

Accuracy and brand voice supersede speed

Speed gains only count if the final content still meets our clarity and voice standards.

Roles this work supports

Writers get faster first drafts, Support gets more findable answers, and Product gets clearer visibility into content coverage gaps — all through the same underlying tagging and AI-assisted workflow.

Impact

Stronger AI readiness through better documentation

  • Reduced first-draft turnaround time with human-in-the-loop review.
  • Improved tagging consistency to support AI and human search.
  • Created team guidelines so other writers could adopt AI tools responsibly.
Reflection

What this taught me

AI tools are only as good as the editorial process around them. The real work was designing guardrails — review steps, tagging standards, and team guidelines — so speed never came at the cost of trust.

"AI should make good editorial judgment faster to apply, not something we bypass."
