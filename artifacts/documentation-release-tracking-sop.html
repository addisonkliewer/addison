Artifact Story

Situation

Frequent product releases affected many help articles, mobile guides, release notes, troubleshooting pages, and release hubs. The team needed visibility into article ownership, drafting, review, publication, and final changes.

Artifact Story

Task

I needed to document a repeatable process that could manage release work at the individual article level while still giving managers and partner teams a clear view of the complete release.

Artifact Story

Action

I defined a hierarchy of release epics, initiative epics, and article tasks. I documented required task information, workflow statuses, assignment rules, release planning, peer and SME review, publishing, audit summaries, reporting, and the definition of done. I also identified future improvements such as custom fields, dashboards, and more formal scope tracking.

Artifact Story

Result

The SOP creates a shared operating model for release documentation. It improves article level accountability, makes blocked or unreviewed work visible, supports more predictable publishing, and preserves a lightweight audit history for every changed article.

This sample is adapted for public portfolio use with fictional products, releases, and examples. It shows a recreated process diagram, a sample article task, and an abbreviated definition of done — not the complete SOP.
