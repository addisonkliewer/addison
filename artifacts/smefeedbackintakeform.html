Artifact Story

Situation

Documentation requests often arrived through messages, meetings, comments, and incomplete tickets. Writers then had to determine what changed, who was affected, where the source material lived, who could validate the work, and whether the request was connected to a release.

Artifact Story

Task

I needed to design an intake form that captured enough information for accurate triage without requiring requesters to understand the technical writing process.

Artifact Story

Action

I structured the form around the product change, user impact, current and expected behavior, source of truth, screenshots, timing, review requirements, risk, customer communication, affected assets, and acceptance criteria. I also proposed consistent labels and a corresponding Jira workflow.

Artifact Story

Result

The form converts loosely defined feedback into actionable documentation work. It improves scope clarity, reviewer accountability, release readiness, risk visibility, and reporting across requests.

This sample was recreated for public use without internal templates or company workflow details. It shows a visual map of the intake categories, a few representative fields, and the proposed Jira workflow — not the complete form.
