Artifact Story

Situation

Documentation teams were beginning to use AI for research, content planning, release analysis, drafting, and knowledge base comparison.

The tools could help writers review more information and move through repetitive work faster, but they also introduced serious risks. Incomplete source material could produce confident but inaccurate output. Existing documentation could be contradicted without evidence. Product details could be invented, confidential information could be entered into unapproved tools, and generic drafts could appear complete before they had been properly reviewed.

The team needed a shared workflow that encouraged useful AI adoption without treating AI output as verified documentation.

Artifact Story

Task

I needed to create a clear AI assisted documentation process that showed writers what they could delegate, which sources they could trust, and where human review was still required. The process also had to work for both new and experienced writers while keeping the writer responsible for accuracy and quality.

Artifact Story

Action

I organized the workflow around the full documentation process, from research through publication. Writers first gather trusted sources, review existing guides, and identify missing or conflicting information before using AI.

AI can help summarize research, compare product changes with existing content, suggest documentation gaps, and draft questions for SMEs. Writers then review those suggestions before using them. New writers may start with summaries and outlines, while experienced writers may create a full first draft when the source material is reliable.

I also added guardrails to prevent AI from inventing procedures, labels, permissions, or product details. Missing information must stay visible as a question, placeholder, or review note. The writer checks accuracy, a peer reviews clarity, and the SME confirms technical behavior.

Artifact Story

Result

The workflow gives writers a faster and more consistent way to use AI without lowering documentation standards. Writers can spend less time sorting through sources and creating early drafts, while reviewers and SMEs can focus on the questions that still need human judgment.

It also reduces the risk of invented details, conflicting language, weak content, and confidential information being used incorrectly. AI supports the work, but people remain responsible for what gets published.
